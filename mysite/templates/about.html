About the Database

Research Platform for the UN Human Rights Framework

A comprehensive digital research tool designed specifically for human rights professionals, legal scholars, researchers, and practitioners working with UN Treaty Body and HRC Special Procedures documents.

Professional Research Platform

This web application provides a unified and intuitive interface for exploring and analyzing UN human rights documents. Designed with human rights professionals, academics, and legal practitioners in mind, it bridges the gap between complex UN outputs and accessible, research-ready information.

The platform includes a comprehensive, searchable database of UN Treaty Body General Comments and Recommendations. With more than 180 authoritative interpretations of international human rights treaties, users can efficiently identify and analyze guidance on the scope and implementation of treaty obligations.

In addition, the database incorporates thematic reports submitted by the Human Rights Council’s Special Procedures. These independent experts provide in-depth analyses and recommendations on urgent human rights issues, allowing users to track evolving standards and practices across the UN human rights framework.

Professional Features

Advanced Search Capabilities

Robust paragraph-level filtering by keywords, concerned groups/persons (children, women, indigenous peoples, LGBTI+ persons, etc.), and UN Treaty Bodies (CRC, CEDAW, CESCR, CCPR, etc.).

Text Analytics & NLP

Leveraging NLTK for word frequencies, bigram analysis, custom UN-related stopwords, and advanced search-within-search functionality for detailed content analysis.

Intelligent Highlighting

Context-aware search term highlighting within documents improves readability and enables quick identification of relevant content sections.

Research Export Tools

Export search results to Excel format for offline analysis, citation management, and integration with academic research workflows.
